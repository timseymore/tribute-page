NIKOLA TESLA

"The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine."

Nikola Tesla 1896.

Once, when asked by someone what it felt like to be the smartest man in the world, Albert Einstein said, “I don’t know. You should ask Nikola Tesla.”

A timeline of important events in the life of Nikola Tesla:

  • Jul 10, 1856 - Born into a Serbian family at the stroke of midnight while lightning struck during a summer storm. As the midwife handed over the baby she said "He will be a child of the storm" to which his mother replied "No. He will be a child of light."
  • 1882 - Moves to the US where he works as an assistant for Thomas Edison for a short time before going out on his own.
  • May 12, 1884 - Meets entrepenuer George Westinghouse who bought his AC patents.
  • Mar 15, 1886 - Invents the AC electrical wire.
  • Jul 30, 1891 - Becomes a naturalized citizen of the United States and in the same year invents the Tesla Coil
  • 1893 - Comes up with the idea of wireless power.
  • 1898 - Invents the remote control.
  • 1934 - Despite complaining about the war, invented a Particle Beam weapon, best known as the "Death Ray"
  • 1943 - Italian inventor Guglielmo Marconi was proven to have used 17 of Telsa's patents. They were reversed and given back to Nikola Tesla.
  • Jan 7, 1943 - Found dead in a New York city hotel room in possession of dozens of inventions.
